# 📖 Unit 02: The Data Analyst Professional

This document outlines the professional standards, technical requirements, and legal frameworks relevant to a Data Analyst, using the "Smart Coffee Shop" as a continuous case study.

---

## 🧑‍💻 2.1 The Role of a Data Analyst
A Data Analyst acts as a bridge between raw business data and strategic decision-making. Their primary role is to collect, clean, and interpret data to provide actionable insights.

* **Core Function**: Transforming low-level data (e.g., daily milk waste) into high-level business recommendations (e.g., "Switching to 2L bottles instead of 4L will reduce spoilage by 12%").
* **Key Responsibilities**: Sourcing data, identifying patterns, creating visualisations, and communicating findings to stakeholders like the Coffee Shop Manager.

---

## 🛠️ 2.2 Technical Skills Required
To manage the Smart Coffee Shop's data effectively, an analyst needs a specific technical "toolkit":

| Skill | Purpose in the Coffee Shop |
| :--- | :--- |
| **SQL** | Querying the main database to pull sales records or bean inventory. |
| **Python / R** | Automating the analysis of large datasets (e.g., a year's worth of temperature logs). |
| **Data Visualisation** | Using tools like Tableau or Power BI to show the manager peak footfall times. |
| **Statistics** | Checking if a "sales spike" was a genuine trend or just a one-off sunny day. |
| **Excel** | Performing quick, "ad-hoc" calculations on daily stock deliveries. |

---

## 🚀 2.3 Career Progression Routes
Data analysis is a foundational role with multiple paths for advancement:

1. **Junior Data Analyst**: Focusing on cleaning data and basic reporting (Current Stage).
2. **Senior Data Analyst**: Leading larger projects and mentoring juniors.
3. **Data Scientist**: Using advanced Machine Learning to predict complex customer behaviours.
4. **Data Engineer**: Focusing on the "plumbing"—building the systems that move data from the coffee machines to the database.
5. **Analytics Manager**: Moving into a leadership role to oversee the entire data strategy for a coffee chain.

---

## 🗣️ 2.4 Importance of Professional Skills
Technical skills must be supported by "soft" skills to be effective in a business environment.

* **Communication Skills**: The ability to explain complex findings (like "standard deviation in bean waste") to the manager in plain English.
* **Critical Thinking Skills**: Not taking data at face value. If the sensor says **0.0g** of coffee was sold, a critical thinker checks if the sensor is broken before reporting a "sales crisis."
* **A Journalistic Approach**: Approaching a dataset like a reporter. Asking the "Who, What, Where, When, Why" to uncover the story behind the numbers (e.g., "Why do oat milk sales peak specifically on Tuesday mornings?").

---

## 🔬 2.5 Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
EDA is the "discovery" phase of a project. It involves summarising the main characteristics of a dataset, often using visual methods, to see what the data can tell us before formal modelling.

* **Coffee Shop Context**: Looking at a month of sales data to see the "shape" of the day—spotting outliers (like a massive £500 order) and checking for missing values.

## 💡 2.6 Importance of Developing and Testing a Hypothesis
A hypothesis is a testable statement used to guide analysis.
* **The Hypothesis**: "Installing a new digital loyalty app will increase repeat customer visits by **15%** within three months."
* **Importance**: It ensures the analysis is objective and focused on solving a specific business problem rather than just looking for random patterns.

## ❓ 2.7 Questions to Ask When Presented with a Brief
When the shop owner asks for a report, a professional analyst asks:
1. **Who** is the target audience (The Manager or the Investors)?
2. **What** specific business problem are we trying to solve?
3. **What** is the timeframe of the data (e.g., last month vs. last year)?
4. **Where** is the raw data stored (e.g., Till system or IoT grinder logs)?

---

## ⚖️ 2.8 What is Personal Data?
Personal Data is any information that relates to an **identified or identifiable** living individual.
* **In the Coffee Shop**: Customer names on loyalty accounts, email addresses for digital receipts, and CCTV footage.
* **Non-Personal**: The price of a latte or the weight of a bean bag (**452.7g**) is not personal data.

## 🛡️ 2.9 Purpose of the GDPR
The **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)** and the **UK Data Protection Act 2018** exist to give individuals control over their personal data and to ensure organisations handle that data responsibly and transparently.

## 📜 2.10 Key GDPR Principles
* **Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency**: We must have a legal reason to collect customer data and be honest about how we use it.
* **Purpose Limitation**: Data collected for "Digital Receipts" cannot be used for "Marketing" without separate consent.
* **Data Minimisation**: We only collect what is strictly necessary. We don't need a customer's date of birth just to sell them a coffee.
